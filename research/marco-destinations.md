# Italy Trip 2026 — Marco's Local Insider Report
**Group:** 2 couples, 4 people, one car, driving from Prague  
**Duration:** 8–10 days total (including drive), so ~6–7 days on the ground  
**Style:** 50% sea/relaxation + 50% photogenic viewpoints. NO history, NO castles, NO museums.  
**Constraint:** Photogenic spots within 1–1.5h of base.  
**Date:** August 2026

---

## 1. RECOMMENDED BASE STRATEGY

### The Verdict: Two bases, south-to-north order

Coming from Prague via Parma/A15 motorway, you drop straight into La Spezia/Levanto first — no wasted mileage. Then shift north to the Portofino/Santa Margherita Ligure zone for the second (longer) base. Zero backtracking.

---

### BASE 1 (2 nights): Levanto
**Why Levanto, not La Spezia or the CT villages:**

- La Spezia is industrial. Fine for parking/transit, ugly to sleep in.
- CT villages (Vernazza, Manarola) are overrun August = €150–250/night, tiny rooms, chaos on every street corner.
- Levanto is 1 stop on the train from Monterosso (4 min), gives direct train access to all 5 villages, has its OWN sandy beach, lower prices (€80–120/night), proper restaurants, and you can actually walk around without being pinned against a wall.
- From Levanto you can also reach Portovenere, Tellaro, and Punta Bianca by car.

**Order logic:** Prague → A1 → Parma → A15 → La Spezia exit → Levanto. Roughly 10h drive. You arrive, dump the car at Levanto's parking, and don't need it again for 2 days.

**What to do from Levanto base:**
- Cinque Terre day (train, early)
- Portovenere + Tellaro + Punta Bianca day (car, early morning)

---

### BASE 2 (4–5 nights): Santa Margherita Ligure
**Why SMdL, not Portofino or Rapallo:**

- Portofino overnight = €400–1,200/night. Absurd.
- Santa Margherita Ligure (SMdL) is the classic Italian compromise: a real town with supermarkets, parking, restaurants, mid-range accommodation (€100–180/night), 5 min by bus to Portofino.
- Boat access to San Fruttuoso, Portofino, and Camogli all from SMdL harbour.
- Sestri Levante (Baia del Silenzio) is 30 min north by car — easy day trip.
- Framura/Bonassola on the way from Levanto — tick them on the transit day.

**Driving Levanto → SMdL:** 45 min on the A12 coast motorway.

## 2. PHOTOGENIC SPOTS — 11 picks, all within 1.5h of base

### FROM LEVANTO BASE

---

#### 1. Manarola Viewpoint (Nessun Dorma)
**What makes it photogenic:** THE iconic Cinque Terre shot. Manarola's coloured tower houses stacked above the harbour, with the vine-covered hillside and glittering sea behind. The best-known "postcard" image of all Italy's coastline.

**Drive/train:** Levanto → train 15 min → walk 5 min uphill to Nessun Dorma restaurant viewpoint.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/7q4aUaMgZMGTkfGT7 (Manarola village) — the viewpoint is along the footpath toward the cemetery, clearly signed.

**Cost:** Cinque Terre Trekking Card required to access trails. 1-day adult = €7.50 (GREEN/off-peak) to €15 (RED/peak). Dynamic pricing — August weekends likely RED. Train card (Treno MS) = €19.50–29.50/day if you want unlimited trains between villages.  
Source: [lecinqueterre.org 2026](https://www.lecinqueterre.org/eng/cinqueterrecard.php) — CONFIRMED

**Best time:** Sunset. The sun drops into the sea to the west and turns the village amber. Arrive 1h before sunset to get position. Sunrise also works (village glows pink, zero crowds).

**August note:** This viewpoint is the most photographed spot in Italy. In August expect 50+ people queuing for the same shot between 10am–6pm. Go at 7am or stay for sunset. Book the famous Nessun Dorma restaurant weeks in advance.

---

#### 2. Vernazza from the Castle Hill (Belvedere)
**What makes it photogenic:** Looking DOWN into Vernazza's perfect horseshoe harbour — the tower, the piazza, the coloured buildings, surrounded by terraced vines. No other village has this combination of foreground/background.

**Drive/train:** Levanto → train 10 min → walk 10 min from Vernazza station up steps toward the trail.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/FhYJPa2UWK3D6FXTA8 (Vernazza)

**Cost:** Trekking Card required (same as above). The belvedere above the harbour is accessed via the trail start — no extra fee beyond the card.

**Best time:** Late afternoon golden hour (4–7pm) when the western light fills the harbour. Morning works but you're shooting into the sun.

**August note:** Platform gets dangerously crowded 11am–5pm (cruise ship waves). Arrive on the 8am train from Levanto, photograph, have a coffee, leave by 10:30am before the hordes.

---

#### 3. Via dell'Amore (Riomaggiore → Manarola)
**What makes it photogenic:** Clifftop path literally carved into the rock face above the Ligurian Sea. Turquoise water 30m below, coloured Riomaggiore visible behind you. Tunnel portals, cactus, sea — very different energy from the village shots.

**Drive/train:** Levanto → train 20 min → Riomaggiore. The path starts at the south end of the village.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/2EuZHBGbPqLQ7F7J6 (Via dell'Amore start, Riomaggiore)

**Cost:** Trekking Card + Via dell'Amore supplement = €10 extra on top of the card. Total ~€17.50–25 adult.  
Source: [cinqueterreriviera.com](https://cinqueterreriviera.com/cinque-terre-card-guide/) — CONFIRMED

**Best time:** Before 8am or after 6pm. Path is short (1.1km, 30 min) — easy to time it.

**August note:** Maximum capacity imposed. Book your time slot online when booking the card — slot fills weeks ahead in August.

---

#### 4. Portovenere — Church of San Pietro on the Rocks
**What makes it photogenic:** The black-and-white striped church perched on a narrow rocky promontory, sea crashing on both sides, coloured village houses behind it. Completely different palette from CT — darker, more dramatic. Byron swam here. The rock arch "Grotta Arpaia" below is pure drama.

**Drive/train:** Car from Levanto = 45 min. Or: La Spezia → ferry/bus from La Spezia Centrale. Ferry from La Spezia harbour takes 30 min — far nicer approach.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/ogjGKSqbM4k9d3xq8 (Portovenere)

**Cost:** Free to walk around. Ferry La Spezia–Portovenere return ~€14/person ESTIMATE (check Navigazione Golfo dei Poeti for 2026 rates). Parking in Portovenere itself very limited — come by ferry or park in La Spezia.

**Best time:** Morning (8–10am) for front-lit church. Sunset for glow on the village facade. August 15 Ferragosto: candle procession lights up the cliffs and church at night — spectacular if you're there, but extremely crowded.

**August note:** Goes from quiet-ish to rammed from 11am. Combine with early morning to beat the heat.

---

#### 5. Tellaro — Village on the Cliff
**What makes it photogenic:** Tiny mediaeval fishing village literally built on rocks above the sea. Pastel houses, octopus drying on hooks, narrow alleys with sea views through every gap. One of Italy's "most beautiful villages" lists. Completely uncommercialized compared to CT.

**Drive:** Car from Levanto = 45 min (via La Spezia).

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/G1ZK8rJa1dB5LKXZ7 (Tellaro)

**Cost:** Free. Park in Lerici (€1–2/h) and walk the coastal path to Tellaro (40 min) OR park in Fiascherino and walk 15 min.

**Best time:** Morning, before 10am, when light hits the east-facing facade. Or late afternoon. Midday light is harsh on the stone.

**August note:** Quieter than CT — locals know it but it hasn't exploded. Best kept "secret" on the Ligurian coast. Go here for the REAL contrast to Cinque Terre.

---

#### 6. Punta Bianca — White Cliff Beach
**What makes it photogenic:** Chalky white limestone cliffs meeting turquoise sea. Looks like a miniature Etretat. Remote, wild, no facilities. The beach is white gravel and rock — completely otherworldly. Bunkers from WWII on the path add texture.

**Drive:** Car from Levanto = 50 min (via Ameglia/Montemarcello). Park at Montemarcello village.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/jmapPxrDhnHJrGC26 (Montemarcello, start point)

**Access:** Hike trails 446 + 444 from Montemarcello — roughly 1.5h each way, steep and over sharp rock in places. A rope is required on one section. NOT suitable in beach flip-flops — proper shoes required. Access can also be by boat/kayak from Bocca di Magra (easier in August heat).  
Source: [sandee.com](https://sandee.com/italy/liguria/bocca-di-magra/punta-bianca) — CONFIRMED

**Cost:** Free. No ticket. Parking in Montemarcello free or €1/h.

**Best time:** Arrive early (before 9am) — the hike takes 1.5h so start walking by 7:30am to be there by 9am in best light and before August heat becomes brutal (can hit 35°C+).

**August note:** Access confirmed but check local trail status (trails.montemarcello.it or ask in Ameglia tourist office). Wild and remote — bring water, hat, shoes.

---

#### 7. Carrara Marble Quarries — Cava di Marmo Fantiscritti
**What makes it photogenic:** White moonscape in the Apuan Alps. The quarries turn entire mountainsides bright white with dust. Roads cut into the rock, giant marble blocks, trucks — alien industrial scale. Zero history-museum vibe — this is raw landscape photography.

**Drive:** Car from Levanto = 40 min.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/sBXC8JFKC3X3UZeN9 (Cava Fantiscritti, main quarry viewpoint area)

**Cost:** You can drive up and view from the road FREE — no ticket needed to photograph from public roads/overlooks. Jeep tours cost €15 (standard, 1h) to €65 (longer/deeper access). Book tours via [getyourguide.com Carrara](https://www.getyourguide.com/carrara-l151014/).  
Source: [theflashpacker.net](https://www.theflashpacker.net/visiting-carrara-marble-quarries-italy/) — CONFIRMED

**Best time:** Morning (9–11am) — white marble blindingly bright at midday, better with directional morning light. Less photography competition than CT.

**August note:** Tours run year-round. No summer closures. Wear closed shoes — marble dust gets everywhere. Bring sunglasses, the white reflectivity is intense.

### FROM SANTA MARGHERITA LIGURE BASE

---

#### 8. Portofino — Piazzetta and Lighthouse Viewpoint
**What makes it photogenic:** The tiny harbour with rainbow-coloured buildings, expensive yachts, and the Castello Brown rising above — all framed by a pine-clad hillside. The lighthouse (Faro di Portofino) viewpoint gives a wider shot showing the full headland and sea. Instagram saturation notwithstanding, it earns its reputation.

**Drive/bus:** SMdL → bus S (10 min, runs constantly) OR taxi boat from SMdL harbour (15 min on the water — far better for photos). NO private cars allowed in Portofino itself — car parks outside fill by 10am.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/hNSfBGeTbfyiQUFB8 (Portofino Piazzetta)  
Lighthouse viewpoint: https://maps.app.goo.gl/5vXvCp5X4JxKH1ct8

**Cost:** Bus €1.50/person from SMdL. Taxi boat from SMdL ~€10–15/person one way ESTIMATE. Walking into Portofino: free. Lighthouse path: free (30 min walk from piazzetta through pines, stunning).

**Best time:** Arrive 9–10:30am (before cruise crowds hit noon–3pm). Evening after 6pm is also golden — yachts lit up, light on facades.

**August note:** Absolutely rammed 11am–5pm. Do NOT drive here — it's practically impossible to park and you waste 2 hours. Boat from SMdL or bus. Portofino's piazzetta in peak August is claustrophobic; the lighthouse walk is the escape and has better views anyway.

---

#### 9. San Fruttuoso — Hidden Cove by Boat
**What makes it photogenic:** Ancient abbey sitting directly above a secluded pebble beach, only reachable by boat or a 2h hike. The cove is ringed by pine trees and cliffs. One of those places that genuinely feels impossible to access by road — because it is.

**Boat:** SMdL → boat from SMdL harbour operated by Servizio Marittimo del Tigullio. Journey ~40 min.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/PWVM5GrtNVwXHmAw9 (San Fruttuoso)

**Cost:** Boat round-trip SMdL–San Fruttuoso ~€20–25/person ESTIMATE (check [traghettiportofino.it](https://traghettiportofino.it/en/)). No entry fee to beach. Abbey entrance ~€5 but you don't need to go in.

**Best time:** Take the first boat out (usually 9am from SMdL). The cove gets crowded fast. Leave by noon or early afternoon.

**August note:** In August the beach is full. Go for the scenery and the boat approach — that's where the photos are. Water is clean and clear. Lounger rental available on the beach if you want.

---

#### 10. Sestri Levante — Baia del Silenzio
**What makes it photogenic:** A small crescent of sand completely enclosed by pastel Ligurian houses, with the peninsula of Sestri Levante rising behind. The houses reflect in the calm water. Only 300m long, intimate scale. Called "Baia del Silenzio" (Bay of Silence) because it's sheltered from the wind and almost perfectly still.

**Drive:** SMdL → 30 min north on A12.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/F6GUzLzfbN6qFmzM8 (Baia del Silenzio)

**Cost:** Public beach — free access. Small parking fee in town.

**Best time:** Sunrise or sunset — the light on the pastel buildings is extraordinary. Morning: zero people. August midday: packed with Italian families.

**August note:** Mostly local Italian crowd (not international tourist hordes). Nicer vibe than CT in August. Beach is tiny — arrive early to swim, shoot in the light, leave before 11am rush.

---

#### 11. Framura / Bonassola Coastal Bike Path (or walk)
**What makes it photogenic:** A converted railway line runs through tunnels along the cliff between Framura and Bonassola, opening onto sea views between rocky headlands. Small coves, clear water, coloured pebble beaches between the tunnel exits. Quiet, local, completely different scale from CT.

**Drive:** Levanto → Bonassola = 20 min (logical stop on the way from Levanto to SMdL). Or SMdL → 40 min back south.

**Google Maps:** https://maps.app.goo.gl/Rq3MNeDxhPHG8kq28 (Bonassola)  
Framura station (elevator to beach): https://maps.app.goo.gl/e9w7h3cXJrSQvv9N6

**Cost:** Free path, free beach access. Parking in Bonassola €1–2/h.

**Best time:** Morning before 10am. Late afternoon. The enclosed coves are best in early light.

**August note:** Busier than it used to be but nowhere near CT levels. Full of North Italian families — that means good food, normal prices, no tourist menus.

## 3. BEACH REALITY CHECK

### Best beaches for the relaxation half

#### Levanto Beach (Base 1)
- Long, sandy (unusual for Liguria), faces west.
- Mix of stabilimenti (private) and free sections. The free section (south end) is wide and clean.
- Water quality: clean, Blue Flag rated historically. Good visibility.
- **Cost:** Free section: nothing. Stabilimento with umbrella + 2 sunbeds August peak: €30–50/day ESTIMATE. Not the VIP stuff — decent mid-range.
- **Verdict:** Best beach for this trip because it's your backyard. No day-trip effort needed.

#### Monterosso al Mare (CT village with the actual sandy beach)
- The only real sandy beach in all 5 CT villages.
- Split: north section public/free, south section stabilimenti.
- **Stabilimento cost August:** €40–60/day for umbrella + 2 sunbeds ESTIMATE.
- Water: clear, clean. Good for swimming.
- Train: 4 min from Levanto.
- **August note:** Still crowded but you escape the village chaos by just lying on the beach.

#### Paraggi (near Portofino)
- A tiny jewel of a cove, turquoise water, directly on the road between SMdL and Portofino.
- Mix of one big stabilimento and small public section.
- Water quality: exceptional — some of the cleanest water on the coast.
- **Cost:** The stabilimento here is EXPENSIVE — €80–100/day per pair of sunbeds is not impossible in August. Public section: free but small and taken by 9am.
- **Verdict:** Beautiful for a photo but plan to swim not set up camp.

#### SMdL Town Beach
- Local beach, convenient, pebble, decent water.
- Stabilimenti along the front: €30–50/day mid-range.
- Free sections available but narrower.

### Versilia Coast Reality (Forte dei Marmi area)
If you had included Versilia (Forte dei Marmi/Lido di Camaiore) in the plan:
- **Forte dei Marmi stabilimenti August:** Standard bagno = €35–50/day for umbrella + 2 sunbeds. VIP establishments (Twiga etc.) = €600–1,500/day per "tenda". Not needed.
- Wide flat sandy beaches, good for relaxing, shallow water.
- **Verdict for your trip:** Skip it. Versilia is flat, suburban, and photogenic only at sunset. The Ligurian Riviera delivers both sea AND views. Going to Forte dei Marmi wastes a base change and adds inland Tuscany driving without photogenic payoff.

## 4. AUGUST REALITY CHECK — FERRAGOSTO AND CROWDS

### Ferragosto (August 15)
- August 15 is a national public holiday. The entire country goes to the beach.
- **Cinque Terre:** Unbearably crowded. If Aug 15 falls during your CT day, STAY AWAY. Go to Tellaro or Portovenere instead — Italian holiday crowds go to CT and Portofino.
- **Trains:** Packed solid on Aug 14–16. Book Cinque Terre Express in advance or go very early (6:30am).
- **Ferries:** Sell out. Book online ahead.
- **Restaurants:** Close in major cities but coastal spots STAY OPEN (it's their peak business week). No closure problem on the Riviera.
- **Fireworks/events:** Portovenere has its famous Madonna candle procession Aug 15 evening — photogenic but very crowded. Worth seeing if your timing works.

### When Day-Trips Are Unbearably Crowded
| Spot | Peak Crowd Window | Your Move |
|---|---|---|
| Manarola viewpoint | 10am–6pm | Pre-8am OR sunset |
| Vernazza | 11am–5pm | 8–10am train, out by 10:30am |
| Via dell'Amore | 9am–5pm | Book early time slot online |
| Portofino piazzetta | Noon–3pm | Before 10:30am or after 6pm |
| San Fruttuoso | 10am–4pm | First boat out (9am) |
| Carrara quarries | Least crowded | Anytime before noon fine |
| Tellaro | Manageable all day | Morning preferred |
| Punta Bianca | Remote — start hike by 7:30am | Beat August heat |

### August Heat
- Expect 30–35°C. Hikes before 9am or after 5pm only.
- The coastal path between CT villages is exposed rock with zero shade — brutal midday.
- Best strategy: photogenic hike/visit in morning, sea/beach in afternoon (3–6pm), dinner and evening walk.

## 5. THREE "WOW" PRIORITY EXPERIENCES

### WOW #1 — Manarola at Sunset (or Sunrise)
This is the #1 photo of the trip. The Nessun Dorma viewpoint path above Manarola, with the village lit in amber below you and the sea beyond. If you only do one thing right, it's this — and you do it at 7am before anyone's there, or stay until sunset while everyone else scrambles for the last train.

**What to book in advance:** Nessun Dorma restaurant for sunset aperitivo — reserve 4–6 weeks ahead for August.

### WOW #2 — San Fruttuoso by Boat from SMdL
This experience can't be replicated anywhere else: arriving by boat to a secret cove with an ancient abbey that has no road access. The approach from the sea — pine-covered cliffs opening up to reveal a little beach and an impossible building — is one of those "did this really just happen" Italy moments. For photos: shoot from the boat as you enter the cove.

**What to book in advance:** Check ferry schedule at [traghettiportofino.it](https://traghettiportofino.it/en/). Buy tickets day before or morning of at the harbour.

### WOW #3 — Carrara Marble Quarries at 9am
Nobody expects this on a sea trip and it completely breaks up the postcard-village pattern. White mountain, white roads, white dust, blue sky. The contrast is surreal. A half-morning there and you drive back to the beach by lunch. Total visual schizophrenia — and that's exactly what makes it memorable.

**What to book in advance:** Self-drive/viewpoint is spontaneous, no booking. Jeep tour: book 1–2 weeks ahead via GetYourGuide.

## 6. SUGGESTED SKELETON ITINERARY (6 nights on ground)

| Day | Base | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 (drive) | Prague → Levanto | ~10h drive, arrive evening. Park car, done. |
| Day 1 | Levanto | Levanto beach morning. Afternoon: walk Bonassola tunnel path (20 min drive). Easy landing day. |
| Day 2 | Levanto | CINQUE TERRE day. 7am train. Vernazza (8–10am) → boat to Manarola → Nessun Dorma sunset. Back by 8pm. |
| Day 3 | Levanto → SMdL | Morning: Portovenere + Tellaro by car (early). Afternoon drive to SMdL via Punta Bianca quick stop or Carrara. |
| Day 4 | SMdL | Full beach day at Paraggi or SMdL. Evening: Sestri Levante (Baia del Silenzio at sunset). |
| Day 5 | SMdL | Portofino morning (boat from harbour). San Fruttuoso afternoon (or same boat runs on). |
| Day 6 | SMdL | Carrara quarries morning. Beach afternoon. Final aperitivo on SMdL promenade. |
| Day 7 (drive back) | SMdL → Prague | Leave early. ~10h. |

Flexible: swap Day 5/6 based on weather. If a day is overcast → Carrara (dramatic clouds). If blazing sun → beach/boat.
