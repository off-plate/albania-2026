# Italy Road Trip 2026 — Logistics Brief
**Prepared by HANS | Updated: June 2026**
**Vehicle:** Volvo XC90 petrol | 4 people | Prague -> Versilia coast (Forte dei Marmi / Viareggio) and Cinque Terre day-trip option

---

## 1. RECOMMENDED ROUTE

**Prague -> Brno -> Mikulov/border -> Vienna ring -> Linz -> Salzburg -> Innsbruck -> Brenner Pass -> Trento -> Verona -> Parma (A22 exit) -> La Spezia (A15) -> Viareggio/Forte dei Marmi (A12)**

**Total one-way: ~970 km | ~10.5–11 hrs pure driving time (no stops)**

Real Google Maps link (Prague to Forte dei Marmi):
[https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Prague,+Czech+Republic/Forte+dei+Marmi,+LU,+Italy](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Prague,+Czech+Republic/Forte+dei+Marmi,+LU,+Italy)

### Route Alternatives Compared

| Route | One-way km | Drive time | Key pass | Verdict |
|-------|-----------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Via Brenner (RECOMMENDED)** | ~970 km | ~10.5–11 h | Brenner 1370m | Best motorway all the way, lowest altitude for August, reliable |
| Via Tarvisio (A23) | ~1,020 km | ~11.5 h | Tarvisio border | Longer, adds eastern detour, worse connection to Versilia |

**Decision:** Brenner is the clear winner. It's the main Alpine transit corridor, fully motorway, and the Italian A22 feeds directly into the A1/A15/A12 chain to reach Versilia. No mountain drama in August.

## 3. FUEL & REST STOPS — SPECIFIC NAMES

XC90 petrol tank: 71L. At 9.5 L/100km, usable range before reserve: ~650 km. You can do the whole trip on 2 fills max, but 3 planned stops is comfortable for the group.

### Recommended Stop Strategy (one-way Prague to Forte dei Marmi)

| Stop # | Location | Where exactly | Why stop here |
|--------|----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Stop 1** | **Ansfelden Nord — Shell Raststation** (A1, km 171 from Vienna) | Just south of Linz, Austria | Cheap Austrian fuel. ~350 km into the trip. Full Autogrill-style services, Shell station. Perfect first break after Czech border. |
| **Stop 2** | **Weer — Autogrill (A12 Inntal, km 55 from Kufstein)** | Between Kufstein and Innsbruck, Austria | Good mid-point rest. Autogrill Austria brand, fuel available. Last chance for cheap Austrian petrol before Italy. **FILL UP FULL TANK HERE** — Italy motorway fuel is ~12% more expensive than Austria. |
| **Stop 3** | **Trento Nord area — Autogrill A22** | Just north of Trento, Italy | You've crossed Brenner, descended the mountains. Good place to stretch legs before the flat A22 blast to Parma. Don't refuel here if tank is OK from Weer. |
| **(Optional)** | **Campogalliano Est — Autogrill (A22 km 7 from Modena)** | Just before Parma junction, Italy | If you're hungry or low on fuel before the A15 mountain crossing |

### Where Fuel is Cheapest: Austria vs Italy vs Czech Republic

| Country | Euro 95 price (June 2026) | Notes |
|---------|--------------------------|-------|
| Czech Republic | **~EUR 1.67/L** | Cheapest — fill up in Prague before leaving |
| Austria | **~EUR 1.73/L** | Still reasonable. Motorway stations up to EUR 2.15 — avoid those, use major Raststationen |
| Italy (national avg) | **~EUR 1.92/L** | More expensive. Motorway Autogrill stations add EUR 0.15–0.25 premium |
| Italy motorway stations | **~EUR 2.10–2.15/L** | Avoid filling up on Italian autostrada if possible |

**Sources:** fuel-prices.eu (June 8, 2026), globalpetrolprices.com (June 8, 2026), winiety24.eu

**SMART STRATEGY:**
1. Fill up in Prague (cheapest)
2. Top up at Ansfelden or Weer (Austrian average price)
3. **MANDATORY full tank at Weer (last Austria stop before Brenner)** — saves ~EUR 10-12 vs filling in Italy
4. In Italy, only fill at regular local stations if you exit — not at autostrada Autogrill pumps

## 5. OVERNIGHT SPLIT OPTION

### Recommended overnight: Innsbruck, Austria

**Why Innsbruck:**
- ~500 km from Prague (just over halfway at 968 km)
- Beautiful city with a real old town — the overnight becomes part of the holiday, not dead time
- Tons of hotel options, good food
- You wake up, Brenner is 37 km away — in Italy before 9 AM
- August price (budget 2-3*): EUR 90–140 per room per night = EUR 180–280 for 2 rooms

**Alternative overnight: Salzburg**
- ~350 km from Prague (too early to stop — you still have 620 km to go)
- Better as a rest/lunch stop on the one-push option

**Alternative overnight: Trento or Bolzano (Italy)**
- Stop AFTER Brenner — ~650 km from Prague
- Already in Italy, beautiful South Tyrol towns
- Price similar to Innsbruck (EUR 90–130/room)

| Factor | One-push | Overnight split |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Drive time Day 1 | 11–12h with stops | 5–6h to Innsbruck |
| Drive time Day 2 | — | 5–6h Innsbruck to coast |
| Extra cost | — | +EUR 180–280 (2 rooms) |
| Comfort | Tough — 4 people, full day | Much better, especially in August heat |
| Arrival condition | Tired, cramped | Fresh, actually enjoy arrival |
| Lost vacation day | None | You lose half of Day 1 from Prague (early departure mitigates this) |

**August heat note:** Driving through the Alps and Po Valley in August with a full car — the AC will be working hard. The Brenner climb is fine for an XC90 but a 12-hour day in summer heat with 4 people is genuinely fatiguing. The overnight option is worth the ~EUR 230 cost.

## 6. HANS RECOMMENDATION

### One-push vs Overnight: **OVERNIGHT SPLIT — Innsbruck or Bolzano**

For 4 people in August, the overnight split is the right call. Here is the breakdown:

**If one-push:** Leave Prague by 6 AM. With 3 stops of 30 min each, you arrive Forte dei Marmi around 6–7 PM. 12 hours in a car in August heat. You'll be too tired to enjoy the first evening.

**If overnight (RECOMMENDED):** Leave Prague at 9 AM, stop in Innsbruck by 3–4 PM. Nice afternoon walk in Innsbruck old town, dinner, sleep. Leave 8 AM, at Forte dei Marmi by 2 PM. Arrive fresh, ready to hit the beach.

**Cost of overnight split:** ~EUR 200–250 for 2 rooms in Innsbruck. The vacation quality uplift is worth more than that.

**Departure timing for August:** Book the hotel in Innsbruck NOW. August is peak season — budget rooms go quickly. The decent 3-star range (e.g. Ibis Innsbruck, Hotel Grauer Bär) runs EUR 90–130/room in August.
